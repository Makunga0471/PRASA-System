Reflection: Challenges in Balancing Stakeholder Needs

Developing requirements for the Smart Digital Monitoring System for PRASA involved balancing the needs of multiple stakeholders with different priorities. Passengers require real-time information and reliable notifications about delays, while operations managers need accurate monitoring tools to manage train schedules and respond to disruptions. These needs sometimes conflict with the technical limitations faced by IT administrators, who must ensure system performance, security, and scalability.

Another challenge involved balancing system performance with security. For example, implementing strong encryption and secure authentication mechanisms is necessary to protect operational data, but these features can increase system complexity and potentially affect performance.

Scalability was also a key concern because the system must support thousands of users simultaneously, particularly during peak commuting hours. Ensuring the system remains responsive while handling large volumes of real-time data required careful definition of performance requirements.

Overall, the process of requirements engineering highlighted the importance of clearly identifying stakeholder concerns and translating them into measurable system requirements that support both operational efficiency and a positive passenger experience.
